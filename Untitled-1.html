What is Cybercrime?

Defining Cybercrime

Cybercrime is seen as the act of Commiting crimes over the internet or on a computer, which may include fraud, extortion and Identity theft, or a crime directed towards computers or other IST’s, hacking, data breaches and malware.

This is essentially just commiting crimminal offences that already exist on a computer.

What are Some examples of Cybercrime?

There are hundreds of forms of cybercrime, from espionage to Crypto-mining. Some basic examples of this are…

  • Cyber espionage - where an organization, government or group of hackers attempt to interfere, manipulate or access data from crime syndicates, other governments and corporations. [Doesn’t typically affect the typical user, unless there data was leaked]
  • Ransomware attacks - Essentially a program threatening to leak, delete or prevent the user from accessing their information on their computer unless a ransom, typically money, is paid within a certain time period.
  • Identity fraud- Where a group of hackers may attempt to steal personal information to commit further felonies, including identity theft.

    • and

  • Cryptojacking - the act where a person may exploit another person's device with the intention of “mining” for crypto-currencies.
